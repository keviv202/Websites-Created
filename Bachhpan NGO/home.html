“Out his eyes with a dream to be free and fly,
His hands though bruised but his hope still high.”

    The eyes that never saw the sight of playground or school, their dry chapped hands have felt the touch of hammers and rugs in place of toys and books, their hands only knows how to scrub and clean, not to write and draw. Their feet have only felt the prick of thorns and rocks which were meant to be placed in a warm shoe. A child like us who should have gone to school, who should have read the books and eaten ice-cream on a hot summer’s day are pushed in a life that adults live. They are children with stolen childhood...

There are children out there who work 16 hours a day, get trafficked, sleep uncovered, pull bricks weighing double their weights, wash plates and much more. They deserve a better life, a purpose, something they can look up to, and something they can live for. One thing that can induce this, without an ounce of doubt, is education.
